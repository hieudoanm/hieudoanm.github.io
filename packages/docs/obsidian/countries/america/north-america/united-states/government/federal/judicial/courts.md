# Courts

## Supreme Court

- 1 Chief Justice of the United States
- 8 Associate Justices
- Established by Article III of the Constitution
- First convened on February 2, 1790

## Courts of Appeals (Circuit Courts)

- 13 Courts of Appeals (11 numbered circuits + D.C. Circuit + Federal Circuit)
- Hear appeals from district courts within their circuits
- Currently 179 authorized judgeships

## District Courts (Trial Courts)

- 94 federal judicial districts
- Each state has at least one district
- Handle both civil and criminal cases
- Currently 677 authorized judgeships

## Special Courts

- **United States Court of Federal Claims** - Claims against the United States
- **United States Tax Court** - Tax disputes
- **United States Court of International Trade** - Trade and customs
- **United States Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces** - Military justice
- **United States Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims** - Veterans benefits

## Bankruptcy Courts

- 94 bankruptcy courts (one per district)
- Handle bankruptcy cases exclusively
